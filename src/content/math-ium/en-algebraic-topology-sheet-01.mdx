## Problem 1

Let $X$ be a topological space. We want to prove a basic property of continuous maps.

### Solution

Let

$$
f : X \to Y
$$

be a map between topological spaces.

By the definition of continuity, for every open set \( U \subseteq Y \), the preimage

$$
f^{-1}(U)
$$

is open in $X$.

Therefore, to check that a map is continuous, it is enough to check that preimages of open sets are open.
